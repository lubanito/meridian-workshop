# Executive Summary

**RFP #MC-2026-0417 — Inventory Dashboard Modernization**
**Submitted by:** Accenture
**Date:** April 25, 2026

---

Meridian Components has a working inventory dashboard and an operations team that depends on it daily — but the system was handed off incomplete, and the gap between what it does and what your team needs has been widening ever since. The Reports module has known defects your previous vendor never resolved. Your Tokyo team works in English-only views. Your IT team has blocked further changes because there are no tests to catch regressions. And your VP of Operations is waiting on a Restocking feature that still doesn't exist.

We understand the ask: don't start over, fix what's broken, build what's missing, and leave the system in a state Meridian IT can maintain with confidence.

Our approach is sequenced to unblock your team as quickly as possible. We begin with an architecture review — the previous vendor's handoff documentation is thin, and we will not make scope commitments until we have verified the current state ourselves. From there we move in priority order: Reports remediation first (your operations team gets relief immediately), automated test coverage second (IT's gatekeeper requirement, which unlocks safe delivery of everything that follows), and the Restocking view third (the capability your team has been waiting for). Architecture documentation is produced as a byproduct of the review phase, not a separate effort at the end.

We are treating the desired items — UI modernization, full internationalization, and dark mode — as a subsequent phase, scoped and priced separately once the required work is complete.

**Assumptions (pending clarification from Meridian):**
- We will conduct our own audit to identify all Reports defects; we have not assumed access to an existing bug log.
- Phased delivery is acceptable; all required items (R1–R4) will be complete before any desired items begin.
- "Current standards" for the UI refresh (D1) will be defined collaboratively at phase two kickoff.
- There is no hard go-live deadline beyond the response date; we will propose a timeline in §4 of this response.
